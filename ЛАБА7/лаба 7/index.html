What is Skyrim

Skyrim gives the player the opportunity to freely travel around the vast game world, exploring it and independently finding new places and tasks. Skyrim takes place in the fictional province of Skyrim on the mainland of Tamriel on the planet Nirn, two hundred years after the events of the previous game in the series, The Elder Scrolls IV: Oblivion. The main storyline of the game is connected with the appearance of the powerful dragon Alduin in Skyrim; the protagonist, Dragonborn, is tasked with stopping the return of the dragons and slaying Alduin.

Dovahkiin

In Dragon language, "Dovahkiin" is a combination of the words "Dovah," meaning "dragon," and "kiin," meaning "born," or "child" which is typically translated to "Dragonborn," but it can also be translated to "Dragonchild. Interpreted thus, the name reads "Dragonkind Hunter Born," or "Born Hunter of Dragonkind," an appropriate description of what the Dragonborn is destined to do. Races

Bretons

In addition to natural magical talent, even the humblest of Bretons boast high magic resistance.

Imperials

Residents of Cyrodiil, born diplomats and merchants. They are skilled with weapons and magic. Wherever gold can be found, the Imperials somehow manage to find a little more.

Nords

Residents of Skyrim, tall blond people. Strong and hardy, Nords are renowned for their martial talents and low sensitivity to cold.

Redguards

This race originates from the ancient continent of Yokuda, which was flooded by the sea in ancient times. They boast high resistance to poisons.

Khajiit

This is one of the so-called animal races, whose representatives are strikingly different from the rest of the inhabitants of Skyrim. Residents of the province of Elsweyr, smart, fast and agile.

Altmer

They have been known for their proud and arrogant attitude towards other races, primarily human ones, which causes mutual hatred. Altmer are extremely gifted in the magical arts.

Bosmer

The Wood Elves are excellent thieves and scouts, and no one in all of Tamriel can match them with the bow. They have an innate resistance to poison and disease.

Dunmer

The Dunmer are good magicians, also distinguished by a developed skill of stealth. They have an innate resistance to fire and can use the wrath of their ancestors to surround themselves with fire.

Orcs

Representatives of this race are green-skinned, with pronounced lower fangs and pointed ears. Orcs are born warriors and blacksmiths

Argonians

The physiology of the Argonian allows them to breathe underwater, which is possible due to the presence of small gills. Moreover, these reptiles successfully resist a variety of diseases.

Casting spells
